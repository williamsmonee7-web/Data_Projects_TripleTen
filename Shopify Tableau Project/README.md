# **Decoding Shopify App Success: Ratings, Reviews, and Developer Impact**

[See Me Before You Read](https://public.tableau.com/views/Sprint4ProjectTableau/SubcategoryRegion?:language=en-US&:sid=&:redirect=auth&:display_count=n&:origin=viz_share_link)

📊 What the project is (features & functionality)

This project analyzes business performance data using interactive Tableau dashboards to evaluate profitability, advertising effectiveness, product returns, and customer return behavior. The analysis combines regional sales performance, product category profitability, advertising opportunities, and return-rate metrics to identify areas where the business can improve operational efficiency and increase profitability.

🎯 Why it was made (problem it solves)

The goal of the project was to help stakeholders make more informed business decisions by identifying the products, regions, and customer behaviors that have the greatest impact on profitability. By highlighting both high-performing and underperforming areas of the business, the analysis provides actionable insights for improving advertising investments, reducing costly returns, and optimizing product strategy.

🛠️ How it was approached (tools & methods)

The project was developed using Tableau to visualize and analyze business performance metrics across multiple dimensions. Interactive dashboards were used to examine profit by region and product subcategory, identify the most effective advertising opportunities, evaluate products and customers with the highest return rates, and assess the relationship between profitability and returns. Visual analytics techniques were applied to uncover patterns, compare performance across categories, and support data-driven recommendations.

📈 What was found (key insights & findings)

The analysis revealed that product categories such as Copiers, Accessories, and Phones consistently generated strong profits, while categories including Binders, Tables, and Bookcases contributed significant losses. Advertising opportunities in Indiana (October), Vermont (November), and Washington (March) demonstrated the highest potential return on investment. The project also identified specific products and customers with unusually high return rates, highlighting areas for further investigation. Overall, the findings showed that the most profitable business segments combine strong earnings with low return rates, providing a clear framework for improving profitability and operational performance.
